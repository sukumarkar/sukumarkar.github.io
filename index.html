Research and Projects
Master Thesis (Jan 2018 - Sep 2018)
From Motions to Emotions: Classification of Full-body Movements using Deep Learning [paper 1] [paper 2] [code]
  • Conducted under the framework of the EU Horizon 2020 project WhoLoDancE at the InfoMus Lab, Genoa.
  • Created and processed a motion-capture dance dataset with four classes of emotions. Represented this data in unique image-like formats and designed a novel dual input CNN architecture which utilized rectangular kernels to extract more correlations along time. Results were better than SVMs and challenged human accuracy levels.
Dynalithe Systems (Sep 2014 - Feb 2016)
Adaptive Signal Systems for Efficient Traffic Control [video] [press]
  • Prototyped a low cost add-on module for traffic lights to optimize waiting time at traffic junctions. This was accomplished by real-time vehicle counting using computer vision. A proof of concept was run in shadow mode on a traffic junction in Udupi, India.
  • Accolades: Finalists at the Texas Instruments Innovation Challenge 2015 (Top 10 among 3100 teams) | Silver Award at KPIT Sparkle 2016 (Top 8 among 1700 teams) | Had the honor of presenting our prototype to APJ Abdul Kalam, former president of India.
  • Team members include Krishna Kinger, Shubham Verma and Thomas John.
Parikshit Student Satellite (Oct 2012 - Aug 2015)
A research project involving the development of a 2U nanosatellite, by students of Manipal Institute of Technology [website]
  • I focused on designing the cubesat's computer architecture for terrestrial thermal imaging. I also worked in-depth on the thermal imaging payload, encompassing tasks such as calibration, interfacing, compression, and post-processing.
  • Represented my college at IAC 2013 and had the privilege to present numerous papers at various conferences. These papers delved into my research on cubesats, covering topics like computer architecture [paper], fault tolerance [paper], and thermal camera calibration [paper].
